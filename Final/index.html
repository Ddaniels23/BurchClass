The Founding of Argus

The Argus Camera Company was founded in Ann Arbor, Michigan in 1936 as a subsidiary of the International Radio Corporation (IRC), a company originally known for producing radios. Argus was created to diversify IRC’s product line during the Great Depression, and it quickly found success with the release of the Argus A, the first low-cost, mass-produced 35mm camera in the United States. This camera helped bring photography to the American middle class, offering affordability without sacrificing too much quality. Argus's most iconic product, the Argus C3 — introduced in 1939 — became one of the best-selling cameras of all time. The company's innovations and accessible pricing played a key role in popularizing 35mm photography in North America.
